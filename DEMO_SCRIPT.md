# 🎬 CivicPulse — Demo Script

> **Duration:** 90 seconds
> **Target audience:** Google Solution Challenge judges
> **Key message:** CivicPulse predicts community distress **before** it's reported, using passive civic signals and AI.

---

## Pre-Demo Checklist

- [ ] Open [civicpulse18.web.app](https://civicpulse18.web.app) in Chrome
- [ ] Ensure Firestore has seeded data (run `python src/admin/seed_firestore.py` if needed)
- [ ] Have the Flutter mobile app open on a device/emulator (or screenshots ready)
- [ ] Close unnecessary browser tabs and notifications

---

## Act 1 — The Problem (0:00–0:20)

**Open:** Landing page at `civicpulse18.web.app`

**Say:**
> "When communities face slow-moving crises — rising food insecurity, declining school attendance, utility shutoffs — the data sits siloed across dozens of agencies. By the time distress is formally reported, it's too late."
>
> "CivicPulse changes this. It fuses passive civic signals into a real-time Community Stress Score per ward — predicting distress before it's reported."

**Action:** Click **"View Demo Dashboard"** → select **Coordinator** role.

---

## Act 2 — The Intelligence Layer (0:20–0:50)

**Open:** Heatmap dashboard — Google Maps with colored ward circles

**Say:**
> "This is Delhi. Each circle is a ward, color-coded by its Community Stress Score. Green is stable, red is critical."

**Action:** Point out the metric cards at the top (Critical Wards, Avg CSS, Wards Monitored).

**Action:** Click on a **red (critical) ward circle** on the map.

**Say:**
> "Let's drill into this critical ward. The CSS is computed from 6 passive signal sources — pharmacy stock-outs, school attendance drops, utility complaints, food bank demand, health facility strain, and social media sentiment."

**Action:** On the Ward Detail page, point out:
1. The CSS hero score
2. The signal breakdown (radar chart / bars)
3. Click **"Generate Crisis Brief"** (Gemini AI)

**Say:**
> "This crisis brief is generated by Gemini 2.0 Flash. It analyzes the signal breakdown and tells NGO coordinators exactly what's happening and what to do — in plain English."

---

## Act 3 — The Dispatch Engine (0:50–1:15)

**Action:** Navigate to **Dispatch Console** (sidebar).

**Say:**
> "When a ward hits critical, we need to deploy volunteers. The dispatch console uses constraint-satisfaction matching — proximity, skills, availability, and fatigue."

**Action:**
1. Select the critical ward from the dropdown
2. Select relevant skills (e.g., "medical", "logistics")
3. Click **"Find Volunteers"**
4. Show the ranked volunteer cards with match percentages
5. Click **"Confirm"** on the top match

**Say:**
> "One click. The volunteer is notified via Firebase Cloud Messaging. And notice — below CSS 76, human approval is required. Above 76, auto-dispatch is eligible. That's ethics built into the algorithm."

**Action:** Show the dispatch appearing in the **Recent Dispatches** table (real-time Firestore update).

---

## Act 4 — Mobile Volunteer App (1:15–1:25)

**Action:** Show the Flutter mobile app (device or screenshots).

**Say:**
> "On the volunteer side, our Flutter app shows live ward status, incoming dispatch alerts via FCM push, and one-tap accept/decline. Same Firestore data, real-time sync."

**Show:** Home screen → Dispatch notification → Accept action.

---

## Act 5 — Impact & Close (1:25–1:30)

**Say:**
> "CivicPulse directly addresses UN SDG 11 — Sustainable Cities — and SDG 17 — Partnerships for the Goals. It turns passive civic data into predictive intelligence, shifting community aid from reactive to preventive."

---

## The Wow Moments (for judges to remember)

1. 🗺️ **Google Maps heatmap** with dynamically sized/colored circles
2. 🤖 **Gemini AI crisis brief** — live AI-generated narrative from real ward data
3. ⚡ **Real-time dispatch** — confirm a volunteer and watch it appear instantly in the table
4. 📱 **Cross-platform** — same Firestore data, React web + Flutter mobile
5. 🔒 **Ethics by design** — CSS threshold gates prevent auto-dispatch without human oversight
